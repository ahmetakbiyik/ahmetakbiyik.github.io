About Research Teaching Photography CV

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Political Economy and Government at Harvard Kennedy School, with my research primarily centered on migration, social cohesion, and the role of social media in shaping intergroup relations. My work is supported by affiliations with The Weatherhead Center for International Affairs and The Institute for Quantitative Social Science (IQSS) and has received grants from J-PAL at MIT and The Stone Program at Harvard.

My research involves studying disinformation and intergroup relations across various digital platforms, such as Facebook, X, WhatsApp, and Telegram. I conduct survey studies and collaborate with great colleagues on other surveys in diverse regions, including Sweden, Turkey, and Lebanon, focusing on the impact of disinformation on individuals and intergroup relations.

Prior to embarking on my graduate studies, I earned my B.A. in Political Science and Economics at Bogazici University in Istanbul and my M.A. in Political Science at Boston University.
